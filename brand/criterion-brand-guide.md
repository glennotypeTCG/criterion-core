# Criterion Group Brand Guide v0.1

## Status

The Criterion Group is a working name for a formation collaboration. It should not be presented as a formal holding company, parent company, registered entity, investment vehicle, fund sponsor, management company, or operating company unless and until that becomes legally true.

The legal form may evolve. The formation gap is real.

## Brand Center

**Primary thesis**

Too many good ideas die before they become fundable companies.

**Core definition**

The Criterion Group is the working name for a formation collaboration designed to close that gap.

**Governing principle**

Criterion is not defined by ownership structure. It is defined by coordinated formation.

**Operating sequence**

Insight -> Signal -> Formation -> First Capital -> Venture-Backable Company

**Operating maxim**

Validate first. Form second. Fund third.

**Strategic distinction**

The names may evolve. The sequence matters more than the labels.

## Brand Role

Criterion is the collaboration brand for coordinated venture formation. It gives the system a shared language without prematurely claiming legal structure.

Criterion sits above the system narratively, but it should not imply legal ownership or control of the layers unless formal ownership exists.

## What Criterion Is

Criterion is:

- A working name for a formation collaboration
- A shared frame for coordinated venture formation
- A thesis about the gap between insight and fundable companies
- A way to coordinate signal, operators, trust, formation, and first capital earlier
- An architecture brand that explains why the layers should work together

## What Criterion Is Not

Criterion is not, unless later formalized:

- A holding company
- A parent company
- A registered legal entity
- A venture fund
- A fund sponsor
- A management company
- A startup studio
- An accelerator
- A consulting firm
- A securities offering

## Brand Architecture

Criterion explains the sequence. The sub-brands or collaboration layers perform the jobs.

| Layer | Brand / Concept | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Collaboration Frame | Criterion Group | Explains why coordinated formation should exist |
| Signal Layer | Fetch Happens | Tests whether the market cares before build |
| Independent Respondent Layer | Candid Voices | Creates independent market signal so validation is not founder-only |
| Formation Layer | Axiom Foundry | Turns signal into structure, operating cadence, MVP direction, GTM logic, and spinout readiness |
| First-Capital Layer | SwiftVC | Represents the first-capital concept for opportunities that earn the right to move forward |

## Legal / Structure Guardrail

Legal truth trumps brand. Do not imply a structure, fund, ownership relationship, or investment product that does not exist.

When clarification is needed, use:

> Criterion is the working name for a formation collaboration. The legal form may evolve. The formation gap is real.

Do not over-repeat legal caveats. Clarify once, then move forward confidently.

## Audience Close

Criterion has a dual close.

**Idea Principal / Operator Close**

> Bring an Idea

For people with insight, domain knowledge, customer pain, or market patterns that deserve disciplined validation.

**Capital Partner / Collaborator Close**

> Support the Formation Collaboration

For capital partners, operators, strategic collaborators, market validators, and future formation partners who believe the formation gap matters.

## Voice Principles

Criterion should sound:

- Declarative
- Restrained
- Intellectually sharp
- Mature
- Anti-hype
- Structurally clear
- Comfortable with caveats
- Commercially aware

Criterion should not sound:

- Cute
- Loud
- Founder-bro
- Overly legalistic
- Overly abstract
- Too academic
- Like an AI SaaS landing page

## Governance Hierarchy

1. Legal truth
2. Criterion narrative architecture
3. Formation / revenue architecture
4. Sub-brand guidelines
5. Campaign or page-specific copy

Clean rule:

> Legal truth trumps brand. Brand thesis trumps style. Formation architecture trumps campaign copy. Sub-brand voice can flex only inside its assigned layer.
