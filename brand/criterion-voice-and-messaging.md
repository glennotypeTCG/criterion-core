# Criterion Voice and Messaging

## Voice Identity

Criterion should sound like:

- A disciplined formation thesis
- A collaboration architecture
- A commercially aware strategic system
- An institutional-quality but emerging framework

Criterion should not sound like:

- A hype startup
- A loud VC brand
- A startup accelerator
- A consulting agency
- A holding company
- A crypto protocol
- An AI SaaS growth page

## Tone

Preferred tone:

- Declarative
- Structured
- Mature
- Restrained
- Pointed
- Thesis-driven
- Calm confidence
- Operator-aware

Avoid:

- Buzzword overload
- Founder-bro language
- Excessive disruption language
- Overclaiming
- Cute metaphors
- Aggressive sales tone

## Messaging Pattern

Criterion messaging should usually follow this sequence:

1. Identify the formation gap
2. Clarify why traditional venture misses it
3. Explain coordinated formation
4. Explain the sequence
5. Clarify collaboration framing
6. Create dual-close pathways

## Canonical Messaging

### Formation Gap

"Too many good ideas die before they become fundable companies."

### Formation Thesis

"This is not only a funding problem. It is a formation problem."

### Collaboration Thesis

"A formation collaboration should exist."

### Governing Principle

"Criterion is not defined by ownership structure. It is defined by coordinated formation."

### Operating Sequence

"Validate first. Form second. Fund third."

### Structure Clarification

"The legal form may evolve. The formation gap is real."

### Strategic Distinction

"The names may evolve. The sequence matters more than the labels."

## Messaging Hierarchy

Top-level narrative:
- formation gap
- coordinated formation
- collaboration architecture

Mid-level narrative:
- signal
- independent validation
- formation discipline
- first capital

Lower-level narrative:
- specific entities
- legal structure
- economics
- portfolio mechanics
