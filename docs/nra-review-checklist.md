# NRA Review Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before publishing, sending, presenting, or committing any human-facing Criterion Group artifact where belief, trust, alignment, decision, participation, investment, employment, response, or commitment matters.

The goal is not to make the artifact more persuasive in a generic sense.

The goal is to make the artifact phase-appropriate, trust-preserving, cognitively lighter, and more likely to move the right human to the right next state.

## Fast Invocation Test

Invoke NRA if the artifact is trying to make a human move from:

- unaware to aware
- skeptical to curious
- curious to engaged
- engaged to trusting
- trusting to aligned
- aligned to committed
- committed to activated
- activated to partner / advocate / investor / employee / founder / respondent

Do not invoke NRA for purely mechanical edits where no human-state progression is involved.

Use this in PRs, artifact notes, or response summaries when NRA materially shaped the work.

## 1. Audience State

Answer before drafting or reviewing.

- Who is the human?
- What do they already believe?
- What do they not yet believe?
- What are they afraid of?
- What are they skeptical about?
- What is their likely objection if they say nothing?
- What prior context do they have with Criterion or the entity speaking?
- What is the smallest next commitment we are asking for?
- Have we earned that ask?

## 2. Entity Selection

Confirm the right entity is speaking.

| Situation | Likely Speaker |
|---|---|
| Ecosystem narrative, institutional coherence, employee recruiting | Criterion Group |
| Idea Principal demand validation, market signal, founder-facing validation | FetchSignal |
| Respondent invites, surveys, voice collection, research participation | Candid Voices |
| Formation path, venture-backable company creation, founder/operator support | Axiom Foundry |
| Fundraising, first-check capital, LP materials, portfolio construction | SwiftVC |

If a respondent is the audience, default to Candid Voices.

If an Idea Principal is the audience, default to FetchSignal.

If an LP is the audience, default to SwiftVC or Criterion Group depending on whether the asset is fund-specific or ecosystem-level.

## 3. Mode Diagnosis

Choose the current mode.

### Evaluation Mode

Use when the human has not yet voluntarily engaged.

Signs:

- ad impression
- first landing page visit
- cold outbound
- first touch deck
- recruiting page
- respondent invite before click

Review questions:

- Does the first line make the human recognize themselves?
- Is the problem emotionally legible immediately?
- Is there one obvious action?
- Is the artifact about them before it is about us?
- Is the cognitive load low enough for a distracted person?

### Transition Mode

Use when the human has engaged but trust is not yet secure.

Signs:

- booked call
- replied to outbound
- started but did not complete a survey
- asked for more info
- requested a deck
- entered founder intake

Review questions:

- Are we diagnosing before pitching?
- Are we capturing their language?
- Are we clear about what happens next?
- Are we avoiding premature solutioning?
- Would a skeptical person feel respected rather than pushed?

### Trust Mode

Use when the human has voluntarily engaged and is assessing risk, alignment, and relationship safety.

Signs:

- active discovery
- second meeting
- serious founder conversation
- diligence process
- proposal review
- formation terms conversation

Review questions:

- Are we using their words?
- Are we proving we heard them?
- Are assumptions explicit?
- Is the recommendation anchored in their stated outcome?
- Are we avoiding persuasion pressure?

### Institutional Mode

Use when the dominant friction is governance, legal, diligence, underwriting, allocation, procurement, or reputational risk.

Signs:

- LP diligence
- legal review
- investment committee questions
- conflict questions
- redlines
- governance concerns

Review questions:

- Are we calm and specific?
- Have we exposed assumptions?
- Are we answering the hard question directly?
- Are conflicts acknowledged rather than softened?
- Would a sophisticated reviewer trust the discipline?

### Outcome Monitoring Mode

Use after a commitment has been made and the human is watching whether promised value becomes real.

Signs:

- onboarding
- post-investment updates
- founder after acceptance
- portfolio support
- respondent follow-up
- early employee ramp

Review questions:

- Are we measuring against the original outcome?
- Are milestones visible?
- Are delays or risks surfaced early?
- Does the communication reinforce reliability?

### Partnership Mode

Use after value has been experienced and the relationship may expand.

Signs:

- renewal
- referral
- expansion
- second fund conversation
- strategic partnership
- alumni founder advocacy

Review questions:

- Is expansion earned by delivered value?
- Are we respecting relationship history?
- Are we referencing original commitments?
- Are we avoiding cold-start persuasion?

## 4. Cognitive Load Check

Remove before adding.

- Is there one core idea?
- Is there one primary CTA or next step?
- Can the artifact be understood without internal context?
- Are entity relationships explained only as much as needed?
- Are acronyms necessary?
- Are we introducing too many concepts at once?
- Are we using plain language where possible?
- Does each section earn the next section?
- Would the artifact still work on mobile?
- Can the human repeat the point after one pass?

## 5. Trust Check

Trust is more important than polish.

- Are we overstating certainty?
- Are we hiding uncertainty?
- Are we using social proof as pressure?
- Are we making the next step feel safe?
- Are we respecting the human's likely fear?
- Are we acknowledging the real tradeoff?
- Are we using the other party's language when available?
- Are we asking for more commitment than the relationship has earned?
- Would a skeptical but fair person feel manipulated?

## 6. Brand Alignment Check

NRA overlays brand. It does not erase brand.

- Is the correct entity speaking?
- Does the tone fit the entity's style guide?
- If the tone deviates, is the deviation justified by the human state?
- Are visual and copy choices serving the state transition?
- Is style consistency being allowed to override trust or clarity?

Conflict rule:

> Legal and disclosure rules first. Then human trust and cognitive effectiveness. Then ontology and positioning. Then brand. Then style.

## 7. Entity-Specific NRA Checks

### Criterion Group

Use for ecosystem coherence.

Check:

- Is the system simple enough to understand?
- Are the entities connected without creating confusion?
- Does the artifact explain why this exists now?
- Is the point of view sharp without sounding inflated?

### FetchSignal

Use for Idea Principal trust in market signal.

Check:

- Does the copy name the risk of false signal?
- Does it make the idea feel protected, not exposed?
- Does it clarify independent human signal?
- Does it avoid cheap validation language?
- Does it help the person decide, not just buy?

### Candid Voices

Use for respondent trust.

Check:

- Is the purpose clear immediately?
- Is exact time stated?
- Is anonymity stated before the first question?
- Is the language calm, civic, and non-startup-y?
- Does the respondent feel useful, not exploited?
- Does the artifact avoid saying they are validating a startup idea?

### Axiom Foundry

Use for formation trust.

Check:

- Does the founder/operator feel their insight is respected?
- Are formation constraints named clearly?
- Is the path specific enough to reduce ambiguity?
- Are tradeoffs visible?
- Does Axiom feel like formation infrastructure, not consulting theater?

### SwiftVC

Use for capital trust.

Check:

- Are fund economics clear?
- Are dilution and retained ownership treated seriously?
- Are conflicts acknowledged and governed?
- Is the formation advantage explained without hype?
- Does the LP understand why this is not open-market deal chasing?

## 8. Clarity Tool Selection

Use the right tool at the right moment.

| Tool | Use When | Avoid When |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-Meeting Agenda | A meeting needs a decision | The meeting is exploratory and still needs openness |
| Heard Document | The other party has shared meaningful context | There has been no real exchange yet |
| One-Page Situation Frame | Shared diagnosis is needed before solution | You are still trying to earn first engagement |
| Decision Matrix | Stakeholders/options/criteria are complex | The decision is simple and the matrix adds load |
| Mutual Action Plan | Both sides must act to reach commitment | It would feel like a vendor-imposed close plan |
| Assumption Register | Trust depends on visible uncertainty | The artifact is early awareness and too much detail would overload |

## 9. Red Flags

Stop and revise if any of these are true.

- The artifact is company-first before audience-first.
- It uses persuasion pressure in Trust Mode.
- It has multiple equal CTAs.
- It hides the real ask.
- It uses internal language the audience would not use.
- It explains the whole ecosystem before the audience cares.
- It sounds impressive but not useful.
- It confuses style consistency with effectiveness.
- It asks for commitment before creating safety.
- It is trying to close when it should diagnose.
- It is trying to inspire when it should clarify.
- It is trying to simplify by omitting material risk.

## 10. Approval Standard

An NRA-reviewed artifact is ready when:

- the audience is clear
- the current state is clear
- the desired state transition is clear
- the mode is correct
- the primary risk is named
- the next step is earned
- cognitive load is intentionally reduced
- the correct entity is speaking
- any brand deviation is justified
- trust is preserved or increased
