# Evidence Standards

## Purpose

This file defines how Criterion separates evidence from inference across Fetch Happens, Axiom Foundry, SwiftVC, Criterion Group, and NewCos.

## Evidence Hierarchy

| Strength | Evidence Type | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Payment or signed commitment | Customer or investor has accepted economic risk. |
| 4 | Active usage or repeated engagement | The market is behaving as if the problem matters. |
| 3 | Qualified pipeline or serious buyer conversation | There is credible commercial interest, but conversion is not proven. |
| 2 | Direct customer interview evidence | The problem appears real, but behavior is not yet strong. |
| 1 | Opinion, enthusiasm, or internal conviction | Useful input, not validation. |

## Required Discipline

Every recommendation must separate:

1. What we observed.
2. What we infer.
3. What remains unknown.
4. What decision the evidence supports.
5. What the cheapest next test should be.

## Entity Responsibilities

| Entity | Evidence Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Fetch Happens | Validate customer demand and document signal quality. |
| SwiftVC | Underwrite investment readiness, ownership logic, and return relevance. |
| Axiom Foundry | Validate formation readiness and delivery feasibility. |
| NewCo | Produce market, product, revenue, and retention evidence. |
| Criterion Group | Ensure evidence standards remain consistent across the system. |

## Red Flags

- Treating a positive interview as willingness to pay.
- Treating market size as demand.
- Treating founder conviction as evidence.
- Treating a weak signal as an investment mandate.
- Treating investment interest as product-market validation.
- Treating services revenue as proof that a NewCo should exist.

## Standard Recommendation Format

```text
Recommendation: Advance / Retest / Pause / Kill
Evidence observed:
Inference:
Unknowns:
Active constraint:
Next test:
Decision owner:
```

## Core Rule

Customer behavior is stronger than customer opinion. Payment and usage are stronger than narrative. Direct market tests are stronger than internal debate.
