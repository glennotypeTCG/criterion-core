# SwiftVC Dual Close: Idea Principal Narrative

## Purpose

This file is the canonical founder-side narrative for the SwiftVC dual close.

Use it when explaining the offer to an Idea Principal, pre-founder, domain expert, or constrained operator who has received independent validation and is being invited into the SwiftVC + Axiom Foundry formation path.

This language should guide:

- Idea Principal offer conversations
- SwiftVC close process materials
- Axiom Foundry formation offer copy
- Founder-facing website sections
- PACT-adjacent documents where the founder side of the transaction must be clear
- Future ontology or MCP references around the dual close

This file should be read alongside:

- `narrative/swiftvc-lp-point-of-view.md`

The LP-side belief is that better signal before formation can create better companies and enough early ownership for LPs to benefit.

The Idea Principal-side belief is that a validated idea is now valuable enough to protect, but still fragile enough that disciplined formation is worth the dilution and structure.

---

## Canonical Point of View

> **A validated idea is not yet a company. It is a moment of possibility. The next risk is formation: who builds it, what gets built first, how capital gets used, and whether the company becomes fundable before momentum fades.**

## What Has to Be True

> **For the dual close to work, the Idea Principal has to believe Axiom can help turn the idea into a more valuable company than they could create alone, fast enough and clearly enough to justify the ownership and structure.**

## Human Version

This is not just a financing decision.

For the Idea Principal, this may be the first time their idea feels real. Someone has tested it. The market has responded. Outside capital is available. The thing that used to live in their head may now become a company.

That moment is exciting, but it can also feel exposed.

The Idea Principal may reasonably wonder:

- Is this still my idea?
- Am I giving up too much too early?
- Why does the capital come with a formation plan?
- Why can I not just take the money and build it my way?
- What happens if I say no?
- Are these people helping me, or taking control?

Those are fair questions.

The answer should be direct and respectful:

> **The idea is yours. The company still has to be formed. Our offer is to form it with you, not around you.**

The dual close exists because, at this stage, capital alone is usually not enough. A validated idea can still fail from poor sequencing, overbuilding, weak customer focus, unclear leadership, bad early hires, messy fundraising, or a story that does not survive investor diligence.

SwiftVC and Axiom Foundry are not offering a loose check. They are offering a structured formation path.

That structure is real. It comes with dilution. It comes with a six-month formation process. It comes with a capital use plan. It comes with a strong operating partner in Axiom Foundry.

For the right Idea Principal, that should not feel like losing the company.

It should feel like finally giving the company a real chance.

---

## The Dual Close

SwiftVC is closing two commitments at the same time.

| Close | Who Says Yes | What They Are Really Accepting |
|---|---|---|
| Capital close | SwiftVC / LP-backed capital | This idea has enough independent signal to deserve first-check capital. |
| Formation close | Idea Principal / pre-founder | This idea will become more valuable with Axiom than it would if I tried to form it alone. |

The second close is emotional because the Idea Principal is not only accepting money. They are accepting help, structure, accountability, and a different path than going alone.

That needs to be honored, not minimized.

---

## Founder-Facing Explanation

> **This is not just a funding offer. It is a formation offer.**
>
> SwiftVC brings the first outside capital. Axiom Foundry brings the formation system to help turn the validated idea into a company that can be built, sold, staffed, and financed.
>
> The tradeoff is real. You are accepting dilution and committing to a disciplined six-month formation process. The reason to say yes is not that you could never do this alone. The reason to say yes is that you believe the company can become more valuable, faster and with less avoidable waste, through this structure than without it.
>
> If you want to take the idea and form it independently, that is a valid choice. This offer is for the Idea Principal who believes the right operating partner, at the right moment, can increase the odds that the idea becomes a real company.

## Emotional Signature

The Idea Principal should feel three things at the close:

1. **Respected**  
   They brought the insight. They are not being treated like a replaceable lead source.

2. **Protected**  
   The structure exists because the idea has value and the next stage is fragile. The goal is not to take control. The goal is to avoid wasting the moment.

3. **Challenged**  
   Axiom will not simply validate their preferred story. The formation process will pressure-test assumptions, sequence work, and force decisions.

The emotional promise is:

> **You do not have to become a venture-backed founder by guessing your way through the first six months alone.**

The operating promise is:

> **We will help convert the signal into a company, and we will do it with discipline.**

---

## What the Idea Principal Gives Up

Do not hide the tradeoff.

The Idea Principal is giving up:

- Some ownership
- Some autonomy
- Some use-of-capital discretion
- Some sequencing control
- Some operating freedom during the formation window
- Some ability to build purely from personal conviction

That is real.

The offer should never pretend otherwise.

## What the Idea Principal Gets Back

The Idea Principal receives:

- First outside capital
- A committed formation partner
- A six-month operating cadence
- A capital use plan
- MVP, GTM, staffing, and investor-readiness support
- A stronger path from validated idea to fundable company
- A team that has incentive to help the company become more valuable

The core trade:

> **Less solo control in exchange for a better shot at becoming a real company.**

## Internal Spine

> **The dual close works only when the Idea Principal believes Axiom is not taking control of the idea, but helping convert the idea into a company.**

Sharper internal version:

> **The offer only works if 85% of a formed, fundable company feels more valuable than 100% of an under-formed idea.**

Use the 85% / 100% line internally. Be careful using it externally. It is useful as logic, but can sound too transactional if it is the first thing a founder hears.

---

## Messaging Guardrails

Do say:

- This is a formation offer, not just a funding offer.
- The idea is yours, but the company still has to be formed.
- The structure exists because the next stage is fragile.
- The six-month process is designed to reduce avoidable waste and increase fundability.
- Saying no is a valid choice. This is how our system works when we commit capital and formation support.
- We both take risk. We both need the company to become more valuable.

Do not say:

- Axiom controls the company.
- The Idea Principal gives up control for six months.
- The founder must spend the money with us because we require it.
- This is free help.
- This is just consulting.
- You could not do this without us.
- The deal is cheap because the idea is early.

Preferred terms:

- Formation plan
- Formation process
- Operating cadence
- Capital use plan
- Six-month formation window
- Disciplined company formation
- Operating partner
- Milestone-based use of capital
- Venture-backable company

Avoid terms:

- Control
- Forced spend
- Mandatory consulting
- Required services
- Giving up the money
- Axiom tells you what to do
