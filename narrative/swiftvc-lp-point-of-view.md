# SwiftVC LP Point of View

## Purpose

This file is the canonical SwiftVC LP-facing point of view for the Criterion Group system.

Use it when explaining why SwiftVC exists, how it relates to Fetch Happens and Axiom Foundry, and what LPs must believe before requesting a PACT or moving into deeper diligence.

This language should guide:

- SwiftVC LP website copy
- PACT invitation copy
- Investor memo sections
- Criterion Group narrative documents
- Internal messaging reviews
- Future ontology or MCP references around SwiftVC positioning

## Canonical Point of View

> **Most early-stage investors meet companies after someone has already turned an idea into a pitch. SwiftVC wants to be earlier than that, when real market signal can still shape the company itself.**

## What Has to Be True

> **For SwiftVC to work, that early signal has to lead to better formation decisions, better companies, and enough early ownership for LPs to benefit from the difference.**

## Plain-English Meaning

SwiftVC is not saying traditional early-stage investing is broken for everyone.

Some LPs have strong access to good angel deals. Some LPs trust established VC networks. Some LPs have had excellent outcomes in the current system.

SwiftVC is making a narrower and more pointed claim:

> The earliest source of venture advantage may sit before the company looks investable.

That is the gap Criterion is built to attack.

The market usually meets a startup after the founder has already shaped the idea into a pitch, built the first story, and started selling a version of the future. By then, many important decisions have already been made: what problem to solve, which customer matters, what to build first, who should lead, and whether the idea deserves capital at all.

SwiftVC wants exposure before those decisions harden.

## Relationship to Criterion Group

SwiftVC only works inside the broader Criterion Group system.

| Entity | Role | Why It Matters to SwiftVC |
|---|---|---|
| Fetch Happens | Independent signal engine | Tests whether the market actually cares before anyone overbuilds. |
| Axiom Foundry | Company formation layer | Converts validated signal into a structured, venture-backable company. |
| SwiftVC | First-check capital layer | Invests first into the strongest spinouts before the broader market has access. |

The investment edge is not simply proprietary deal flow.

The investment edge is proprietary formation exposure.

SwiftVC is not only seeing companies early. It is investing in companies whose validation, structure, and first operating plan were shaped inside the Criterion system.

## LP Belief Test

SwiftVC is for LPs who believe, or are willing to test, the following:

1. Early-stage venture outcomes are shaped before a company becomes conventionally fundable.
2. Independent market signal is more useful than founder-led conviction alone.
3. Better formation decisions can create better companies.
4. A fund can earn advantage by owning companies before the market sees them clearly.
5. The advantage only matters if SwiftVC owns enough of the winners after dilution.

SwiftVC is not for LPs who believe the best opportunity is always in the open-market angel or pre-seed channel.

That disagreement is acceptable. The point of view should qualify the right LPs and disqualify the wrong ones.

## Messaging Guardrails

Do say:

- SwiftVC wants to be earlier than the pitch.
- SwiftVC is built around independent signal and disciplined formation.
- The fund is trying to improve the starting conditions of venture-backed companies.
- LPs benefit only if signal improves formation quality and SwiftVC preserves meaningful ownership.

Do not say:

- Traditional venture is broken for everyone.
- SwiftVC is smarter than the market.
- Signal eliminates venture risk.
- The Foundry exists to generate service revenue from funded companies.
- Every validated idea deserves capital.

## One-Sentence Summary

> **SwiftVC is a first-check fund for LPs who believe better venture outcomes can begin before the company looks investable, when independent signal can still shape what gets built, what gets killed, and who owns the result.**

## Short Website Version

> **Most investors meet startups after the pitch already exists. SwiftVC wants to be earlier, when market signal can still shape the company.**

## PACT Readiness Version

> **Requesting a PACT means you are ready to evaluate SwiftVC’s core belief: that independent signal and disciplined formation can create better early-stage companies than LPs typically access through normal angel or pre-seed channels.**
