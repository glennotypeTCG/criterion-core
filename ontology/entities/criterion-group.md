# Criterion Group

## Ontology Type

entity-definition

## Entity

Criterion Group

## Canonical Definition

> The Criterion Group is an aligned venture-formation architecture built to create better companies earlier. It connects independent market signal, formation capital, and disciplined company-building support through distinct entities that may work together where appropriate. Its value is not common ownership or a guaranteed pathway. Its value is the structural advantage created when strong ideas are tested safely, funded intentionally, and formed with discipline before the broader market sees the opportunity.

## Plain-English Version

Criterion Group is the coordination layer behind a better path from idea to signal to capital-backed formation.

It helps distinct capabilities work like a system without pretending they are one company, one fund, or one guaranteed funnel.

## Human Version

Most good ideas do not fail because no one cared.

They fail because the path from idea to company is messy, lonely, underfunded, and poorly sequenced.

Criterion Group is built around a better path:

1. Test the idea safely.
2. Commit capital only when the signal is strong enough.
3. Help form the company with discipline before the market fully sees it.

## Strategic Role

Criterion Group exists to create structural advantage at the moment most venture systems are weakest:

- Before the company is fully formed
- Before the pitch is polished
- Before the market has priced the opportunity
- Before capital is deployed into avoidable mistakes

## Coordinated Capabilities

| Capability | Entity | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Independent market signal | FetchSignal / Fetch Happens | Helps ideas meet the market safely. |
| Formation capital | SwiftVC | Can turn strong signal into a capital-backed formation opportunity. |
| Disciplined company-building support | Axiom Foundry | Can make formation more disciplined, faster, and more fundable. |

## What Criterion Group Is

Criterion Group is:

- A coordination architecture
- A shared ontology
- A governance model
- A sequencing model
- A way to align distinct venture-formation capabilities
- A source of structural advantage when the parts work together appropriately

## What Criterion Group Is Not

Criterion Group is not, unless legally documented otherwise:

- A single legal entity representing all capabilities
- A holding company that owns every component
- The owner or controller of SwiftVC
- A fund manager
- A venture fund
- A public-facing customer ecosystem
- A guaranteed funding pathway
- A required funnel from FetchSignal to SwiftVC or Axiom Foundry
- Permission to share customer data, ideas, studies, or recordings across entities by default

## Legal and Governance Bright Lines

### Common Ownership

Do not imply common ownership unless it is legally true.

Preferred language:

> Distinct entities and relationships designed to work together where appropriate.

Avoid:

> Criterion owns FetchSignal, SwiftVC, and Axiom Foundry.

### SwiftVC Relationship

Do not imply SwiftVC is controlled by Criterion unless governance documents say so.

Preferred language:

- Aligned capital relationship
- Formation capital relationship
- Capital-backed formation opportunity
- First-check capital pathway where appropriate

Avoid:

- Criterion's fund
- Our fund, unless legally true
- SwiftVC is owned by Criterion
- Criterion controls SwiftVC

### FetchSignal Customer Relationship

Do not tell FetchSignal customers that they are entering the Criterion ecosystem.

Preferred language:

> FetchSignal may identify optional next-step resources based on the signal and the customer's permission.

Avoid:

> You are now in the Criterion ecosystem.

### Referral and Data Sharing

A referral, preferred provider introduction, or discount code does not mean the customer's full idea, full study, recordings, or detailed results will be shared.

The customer controls what is shared, with whom, and when, subject to written permissions and agreements.

### Funding and Formation

Do not imply a positive signal guarantees SwiftVC funding, Axiom Foundry support, or admission into any affiliated process.

Preferred language:

> A strong signal may create additional options, including independent referrals, formation support, or capital conversations where appropriate.

### Axiom Foundry Role

Do not imply formation is impossible without Axiom Foundry.

Preferred language:

> Axiom Foundry may make formation more disciplined, faster, and more fundable for the right opportunity.

Avoid:

> Axiom makes formation possible.

## Messaging Guardrails

### Do Say

- Coordination architecture
- Distinct but aligned capabilities
- Shared standards and sequencing
- Permission-based relationships
- Structural advantage before the market sees the opportunity
- Tested safely, funded intentionally, formed with discipline
- Designed to work better together where appropriate

### Do Not Say

- Public ecosystem
- Guaranteed pathway
- Commonly owned system, unless legally true
- Criterion's fund, unless legally true
- Automatic funnel
- Required pathway
- Data shared across the system by default
- Every positive signal leads to capital

## One-Line Version

> Criterion Group is the coordination layer, not the legal wrapper.

## External-Safe Version

> FetchSignal, SwiftVC, and Axiom Foundry may work together where appropriate, but each relationship is separate, permission-based, and governed by its own agreements.
