# Thesis Risk Bright Lines

## Ontology Type

governance-primitive

## Purpose

This file identifies legal, governance, and trust risks that could put the core thesis at risk if not handled correctly.

It is not legal advice.

It is a corporate-lawyer-style review of the major places where messaging, contracts, product flows, referrals, investment processes, or founder conversations could accidentally undermine the system.

Use this file when reviewing:

- Public website copy
- Founder-facing offers
- LP-facing materials
- FetchSignal intake and study agreements
- ICP outreach and recording consent flows
- SwiftVC offering and diligence materials
- Axiom Foundry services, equity, and formation agreements
- Referral language
- Data-sharing permissions
- Future MCP or agent workflows

## Core Operating Rule

> Coordinate through standards, not assumptions. Share through permission, not proximity. Invest through documented rights, not vibes.

# FetchSignal: Three Things to Be Careful Of

FetchSignal depends on trust.

The Idea Principal has to believe that FetchSignal can produce credible market signal without exposing the idea carelessly, damaging their reputation, or limiting what they can do next.

## 1. Confidentiality, Idea Ownership, and Data-Sharing Boundaries

### Risk

The thesis breaks if an Idea Principal believes FetchSignal leaked, reused, borrowed, or advantaged someone else with their idea.

FetchSignal asks the person to let the idea leave their head. That creates special trust obligations.

### Bright Lines

- Do not share study outputs, recordings, transcripts, ICP lists, or idea details with SwiftVC, Axiom Foundry, referral partners, or other providers without explicit permission.
- A referral, preferred provider introduction, or discount code does not equal permission to share the study.
- Contracts should clearly address idea ownership, research output ownership, usage rights, retention rules, and anonymization rules.
- Do not imply that using FetchSignal means the customer has entered a public Criterion ecosystem.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

FetchSignal stops being safe first contact with the market and becomes perceived as an idea-exposure risk.

## 2. Interview Consent, Recording, and Outreach Compliance

### Risk

Recorded ICP conversations create consent, privacy, and reputational risk.

Outreach can also trigger spam, solicitation, call-recording, deceptive-practice, and AI-disclosure concerns depending on channel and jurisdiction.

### Bright Lines

- Get clear consent before recording.
- Use compliant, respectful outreach language.
- Do not misrepresent whether a company exists, whether a product is live, or whether the outreach is customer research versus sales.
- Keep AI use transparent where appropriate, especially if AI participates in calls, transcription, analysis, or synthesis.
- Maintain call-recording, data retention, and deletion procedures.
- Keep outreach brand-safe so the Idea Principal is not made to look amateur, spammy, desperate, or unserious.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

One sloppy outreach campaign can violate the brand-safe promise and make the Idea Principal fear exposure more than they value the signal.

## 3. Overstating the Strength or Portability of the Signal

### Risk

FetchSignal may produce useful evidence, but it should not be marketed as a guarantee of product-market fit, funding, SBA approval, revenue, or investability.

### Bright Lines

- Avoid terms like proven, guaranteed, bankable, investor-ready, or validated unless carefully defined.
- Prefer evidence, signal, market feedback, patterns, and decision support.
- Define what positive, neutral, and negative signals mean.
- Make clear that negative signal is not failure.
- Make clear that positive signal is not guaranteed success.
- Do not describe FetchSignal as a guaranteed funding path or automatic funnel into SwiftVC or Axiom Foundry.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

FetchSignal becomes legally and commercially exposed when customers treat the report as a financing, investment, or launch guarantee.

# SwiftVC: Three Things to Be Careful Of

SwiftVC depends on LP trust, clear securities posture, and credible access to capital-backed formation opportunities.

The thesis is that early signal must lead to better formation decisions, better companies, and enough early ownership for LPs to benefit from the difference.

## 1. Securities Offering Language and Performance Claims

### Risk

LP-facing materials can drift from thesis into implied return promises.

SwiftVC should not claim that traditional venture is broken for everyone, that SwiftVC is smarter than the market, or that signal eliminates venture risk.

### Bright Lines

- Do not guarantee returns.
- Do not say SwiftVC will outperform.
- Do not use projected MOIC, IRR, DPI, or markup language casually or without assumptions and disclaimers.
- Clearly distinguish belief, strategy, model assumptions, and actual results.
- Keep offering materials, pitch materials, and public marketing aligned.
- Avoid language that sounds like investment advice outside the proper offering context.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

The LP story becomes legally vulnerable and less credible with sophisticated investors.

## 2. Conflicts of Interest Across SwiftVC, Axiom Foundry, FetchSignal, and Affiliated Economics

### Risk

If SwiftVC capital flows into companies that then pay Axiom Foundry or other affiliated resources, LPs may ask whether the fund is investing for portfolio returns or feeding services revenue.

The system must show that services improve company quality and fund outcomes, not that the fund exists to create internal service revenue.

### Bright Lines

- Disclose affiliated relationships.
- Disclose any required, expected, or preferred use of Axiom Foundry or other affiliated services.
- Make service pricing, deliverables, and approval logic transparent.
- Clarify who owes duties to whom.
- Put conflict review mechanics in fund documents or side materials where appropriate.
- Avoid hidden or circular economics.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

LPs conclude the model optimizes Foundry revenue instead of fund returns.

## 3. Control, Allocation, and Ownership Claims

### Risk

SwiftVC may be an aligned capital relationship, not owned or controlled by Criterion.

If public language implies common control, proprietary obligation, guaranteed access, or automatic allocation, it can create governance and reliance issues.

### Bright Lines

- Define exactly what SwiftVC receives: first look, first right, allocation, pro rata, information rights, or no formal right.
- Do not say Criterion controls SwiftVC unless legally true.
- Do not promise every strong FetchSignal idea becomes a SwiftVC opportunity.
- Be precise about ownership targets versus guaranteed ownership.
- Document any access rights, allocation rights, pro rata rights, or information-sharing rights.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

SwiftVC's structural advantage becomes vague, unenforceable, or misleading.

# Axiom Foundry: Three Things to Be Careful Of

Axiom Foundry depends on founder trust and clean formation mechanics.

The Idea Principal has to believe Axiom can help turn the idea into a more valuable company than they could create alone, fast enough and clearly enough to justify the ownership and structure.

## 1. Avoiding Control Language and Preserving Founder Agency

### Risk

Axiom must make disciplined formation feel protective and empowering rather than controlling.

If the process feels like control extraction, the founder-side thesis breaks.

### Bright Lines

- Do not say Axiom controls the company.
- Do not say the founder gives up control.
- Use formation process, operating cadence, capital use plan, and operating partner.
- Governance documents should clearly state decision rights, reserved matters, service scope, termination rights, and what happens if the relationship breaks down.
- Do not imply the founder could never do this without Axiom.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

The Idea Principal experiences the deal as predatory rather than value-creating.

## 2. Services-for-Equity and Required-Spend Conflicts

### Risk

If Axiom receives fees, equity, or both, and especially if SwiftVC money is used to pay Axiom, the structure needs careful disclosure and fairness.

Axiom's economics must support the claim that it improves formation quality.

### Bright Lines

- Clearly separate investment terms from service terms.
- Define deliverables, milestones, and pricing.
- Avoid implying services are free if paid through invested capital, equity economics, or company obligations.
- Document whether Axiom is advisor, consultant, co-founder, venture studio, service provider, or operating partner.
- Avoid hidden or circular economics.
- Make sure required or preferred spend is justified by company value creation, not internal extraction.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

Axiom looks like a tax on capital rather than a formation accelerator.

## 3. IP, Work Product, Founder Role, and Future Ownership Ambiguity

### Risk

Formation work creates valuable assets: product specs, code, customer lists, brand assets, pitch materials, financial models, workflows, and investor materials.

If ownership is unclear, future financings can become difficult or impossible.

### Bright Lines

- Assign work product clearly.
- Define pre-existing IP versus new company IP.
- Clarify founder equity, vesting, advisor equity, Axiom equity, and any repurchase or reverse-vesting mechanics.
- Ensure contractor agreements include invention assignment and confidentiality.
- Keep cap table and IP clean enough for follow-on investors.
- Make sure future investors can diligence the company without unresolved formation disputes.

### Thesis Risk if Missed

Axiom may create value operationally but destroy fundability legally.

# Cross-System Caution

The biggest shared legal risk is implied integration without documented boundaries.

The system wants to feel coordinated. That is the value creation thesis.

But it cannot sound like:

- One legal entity if it is not
- One automatic funnel if it is not
- One shared data pool if permission has not been granted
- One guaranteed funding pathway if decisions remain discretionary
- One controlled fund if governance documents do not support that claim
- One mandatory services pathway if founders retain meaningful choice

## Universal Bright Line

> Coordinate through standards, not assumptions. Share through permission, not proximity. Invest through documented rights, not vibes.
