# Candid Voices Style Guide

## Status

Active v2.0 conversion from uploaded CandidVoices brand guidelines.

## Purpose

This Style Guide governs Candid Voices respondent-facing, voice-of-customer, interview, recording, and conversational research materials.

The core design principle is respondent-first trust.

Every Candid Voices decision should be filtered through one question:

> Does this make a skeptical, time-pressed, non-expert person feel safe enough to give an honest answer?

## Applies To

- Candid Voices respondent invites
- Survey landing pages
- Completion screens
- Panel membership invitations
- Recorded conversations
- Voice-of-customer content
- Research and storytelling assets
- Interview and respondent experience flows
- Candid Voices visual identity and copy patterns

## Primary Audience

Candid Voices speaks to ordinary people with real-world experience.

They are not expert consultants.

They are not startup validators.

They are respondents whose lived experience can inform decisions being made right now.

## Respondent Archetypes

### The Skeptic

Most common respondent.

Primary fear:

> Is this a scam? Will I get spammed?

Motivation if convinced:

> Mild civic duty, slight curiosity, feeling heard.

Design requirement:

The Skeptic needs legitimacy signals before engaging: real domain, clear purpose, anonymity, exact time, and no scam-coded language.

### The Casual Voice

Digitally fluent and more likely to engage if the topic feels relevant.

Primary fear:

> Will anyone actually read this?

Motivation if convinced:

> Their perspective might actually matter.

Design requirement:

The Casual Voice drops out if questions feel generic or leading. They return if they feel their signal was used.

### The Occasional Expert

Domain-adjacent but not a paid expert-call participant.

Primary fear:

> This is going to waste my time.

Motivation if convinced:

> The topic is genuinely in their world.

Design requirement:

The Occasional Expert needs questions that reflect nuance and respect their time.

## Desired Audience Reaction

Candid Voices should make respondents feel:

- Safe
- Respected
- Heard
- Unpressured
- Anonymous
- Useful
- Part of something consequential

The target emotion is:

> I feel respected and heard.

Not:

> I feel excited.

## Core Positioning

Candid Voices is an independent voice panel.

It is a place where ordinary people with real-world experience contribute honest perspectives on decisions being made right now.

No expertise required.

No identity exposed.

Just a candid view, on the record.

## Central Positioning Insight

Respondents do not need to know they are helping builders validate startup ideas.

The moment they know this, data quality collapses. Social desirability bias floods in, and honest signal becomes noise.

The reframe:

> Respondents do not help builders. They contribute their lived experience to decisions being made about things that affect their world.

### Never Say

> A startup wants to know if you would use their product.

### Always Say

> A decision is being made about [problem space]. We want the perspective of people who actually deal with it.

## What Candid Voices Is and Is Not

| Dimension | Candid Voices Is Not | Candid Voices Is |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | A tool for startup validation | An independent civic voice panel |
| Audience | Expert consultants | Anyone with relevant lived experience |
| Format | Scheduled expert interview or focus group | Async, short, anonymous, no account needed |
| Incentive | Points or gift cards as the lead hook | Consequential contribution, impact first |
| Brand Feel | Survey mill, SaaS tool, market research firm | Civic panel, public radio, Gallup energy |

## Message Hierarchy

Every respondent-facing touchpoint should communicate in this order:

1. What decision or topic is being explored.
2. Why this person's lived experience is relevant.
3. How long it will take.
4. That responses are anonymous.
5. That no account is required.
6. That there is no follow-up unless they opt in.
7. What happens after completion.

## Trust Architecture

Trust is built or destroyed at specific moments:

- Subject line
- Redirect behavior
- First page
- First question
- Time accuracy
- Completion screen
- Follow-up behavior

Every touchpoint is a trust event.

### Builds Trust

- Real domain and About page
- Visible respondent count
- Exact time stated and honored
- Anonymity declared before the first question
- Mobile-optimized design
- Completion confirmation
- Plain-language data policy
- Topic relevance stated upfront

### Destroys Trust

- "You've been specially selected"
- Redirecting to a third-party survey tool
- Demographic interrogation upfront
- Countdown timers
- Asking for email at the end
- "Win $500" as the headline
- Follow-up email within 24 hours without opt-in
- Any mention of startup or builder

## Legitimacy Layer

Minimum viable legitimacy signals before respondent recruitment:

- Real domain
- Real About page
- Plain-English privacy policy
- SSL / HTTPS
- Respondent panel page
- No redirect chains
- Recognizable one-click unsubscribe
- Human contact path

## Copy Rules

### Subject Line Options

Purpose-led:

> 3 minutes on [topic]. Your perspective needed.

Civic-framed:

> A question about [topic] — from people who deal with it.

Direct:

> 4 questions. Anonymous. 3 minutes. About [topic].

Never use:

- You've been selected
- Win $500
- Quick survey
- Help us improve

### General Invite Template

> A decision is being made about [problem space]. Before it is finalized, the organization behind it wants to hear from people who actually deal with this — not advisors, not analysts. People with real-world experience.
>
> This is 4 questions. It takes 3 minutes. Your responses are completely anonymous. No one sees who said what, only what the group said. No account required. No follow-up.

Call to action:

> Share your perspective

Metadata line:

> Anonymous · 4 questions · 3 minutes · No account

### Survey Landing Page Template

Headline:

> Your perspective. On the record.

Body:

> A decision is being made about [topic]. Candid Voices is collecting honest signal from people with real-world experience — not experts, not surveys to friends. You.
>
> Your responses are anonymous. The organization sees what people think, not who said it. 4 questions, 3 minutes, no account required.

CTA:

> Begin

### Completion Screen Template

Headline:

> Your voice has been recorded.

Body:

> You are one of [N] people whose perspective will be reported to inform a real decision. Results will be delivered within [X] days.
>
> No follow-up. No pitch. No further contact unless you choose to join the Candid Voices panel and contribute to future studies.

### Panel Membership Invite Template

Headline:

> Want to keep contributing?

Body:

> Candid Voices runs 2 to 4 studies per month across topics that shape real decisions: healthcare, work, technology, housing, finance, and more. Panel members receive a new question set when their profile matches the study topic.
>
> It is anonymous, async, and always under 5 minutes. No compensation required, though studies that ask for more time occasionally include a modest honorarium.

CTA:

> Join the panel

## Respondent Journey

### Moment 01: The Invite

Job:

> Earn the open. State purpose in sentence one.

Trust test:

> Would a skeptic open this or flag it as spam?

### Moment 02: Landing Page

Job:

> Build trust before the first question. No exceptions.

Trust test:

> Would a Skeptic give honest answers here?

### Moment 03: Question Flow

Job:

> Ask honestly. Never lead. Honor the stated time.

Trust test:

> Could this question appear in a Pew Research study?

### Moment 04: Completion

Job:

> Confirm receipt. Confirm consequence. No celebration theater.

Trust test:

> Does this feel like a real organization or a survey mill?

### Moment 05: Panel Return

Job:

> Build loyalty through impact, not incentive.

Trust test:

> Would an Occasional Expert feel this is worth their time again?

## Absolute Prohibitions

These are not style preferences. They are signal-quality and trust-protection rules.

### Never mention startup, founder, or builder

This reveals the commercial purpose and biases the respondent.

### Never mention FetchHappen or FetchSignal

Candid Voices is an independent respondent-facing brand. Any explicit or implied connection destroys neutrality.

### Never use "You've been specially selected"

The phrase is permanently scam-coded.

### Never say "Help us improve our product"

This frames the respondent as free labor for an unnamed commercial entity.

### Never use leading question framing

Bad:

> How much does this frustrate you?

Better:

> How often do you experience [X]?

### Never use exclamation marks on completion screens

Close with confirmation, not celebration.

### Never lead with incentives

Incentive-first framing attracts low-quality respondents and damages impact-driven retention.

## Legal / Governance Guardrails

- Candid Voices must protect respondent trust before any downstream research objective.
- Respondents should not be told they are validating startup ideas.
- Respondents should not be told about FetchHappen or FetchSignal.
- Anonymity must be stated before the first question.
- Data use must be plain-language and visible.
- No account gate for standard short studies.
- No follow-up without explicit opt-in.
- No third-party redirects for survey completion.
- Honor the stated time and question count.
- Avoid misleading claims about who commissioned the study.
- Use neutral question design.
