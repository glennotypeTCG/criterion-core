## Purpose

This Style Guide governs customer-facing FetchSignal materials.

FetchSignal helps Idea Principals get trusted human market signal before they build too much, spend too much, or ask others to believe too much.

The core design principle is trust before conversion.

## Applies To

- FetchSignal Landing Page
- FetchSignal Sales Deck
- FetchSignal Pitch Deck
- Idea Principal outreach and onboarding
- Signal report framing
- Founder-facing copy
- Methodology and FAQ content
- Mobile-first landing page design
- CTA hierarchy

## Primary Audience

FetchSignal speaks to builders, operators, domain experts, and Idea Principals who suspect validation matters but do not trust normal founder-led validation.

They are not looking for encouragement.

They are looking for truth they can act on.

## Desired Audience Reaction

FetchSignal should make the Idea Principal feel:

- Safe enough to let the idea meet the market
- Respected for their intelligence and skepticism
- Protected from fake validation
- Clear about what happens next
- More confident acting, pausing, or walking away

The target emotion is:

> I can learn the truth without losing control of the idea.

## Brand Philosophy

FetchSignal gives builders what Gallup gives politicians: real signal from real people before the decision is made.

The decision for an Idea Principal is high-stakes:

> Is this idea worth my time, money, reputation, and life energy?

FetchSignal exists to answer that question with independent market signal.

## Brand Anchor

> The market has spoken. Now build with conviction.

Use this as a brand anchor, not as a universal headline.

## Core Pillars

### Credibility

Every claim should be traceable to real respondents and a clear methodology.

Do not fabricate, estimate, or extrapolate beyond what the evidence supports.

If the signal is weak, say so.

### Human Signal

The value comes from real people with no stake in flattering the idea.

Independence is the product.

Protect it in every design, copy, and research decision.

### Decisive Clarity

FetchSignal should not create reports for report's sake.

It should create decision-grade signal that helps the Idea Principal decide whether to continue, refine, pause, or stop.

## Core Customer Archetypes

### Archetype 01: The Constrained Expert

Primary ICP.

Internal monologue:

> I know too much to trust fake signal, but I do not know how to get real signal fast.

Profile:

- Deep domain expertise
- High opportunity cost
- First-time or early builder
- Aware that polite conversations are not demand

Trust language:

- Independent signal
- Decision-grade
- People with no reason to say yes
- Remove bias from the room
- Signal you can act on, or walk away from

Avoid:

- AI-powered insights
- Validate your idea in minutes
- Score your startup
- Any shortcut language

### Archetype 02: The Scarred Operator

Primary ICP.

Internal monologue:

> I have already built something no one wanted.

Profile:

- Repeat founder, product lead, or operator
- Has shipped something that did not land
- Allergic to fluff
- High willingness to pay if the process feels serious

Trust language:

- Leading the witness
- False positives
- Encouragement is not demand
- Kill the idea early, or commit with confidence

### Archetype 03: The Pre-Founder Analyst

Secondary ICP.

Internal monologue:

> I evaluate risk for a living. Why is startup validation so sloppy?

Trust language:

- Probability of demand
- Independent respondents
- Unincentivized signal
- Replace narrative with evidence

### Archetype 04: The Anti-Playbook Builder

High-leverage niche.

Internal monologue:

> I do not trust startup advice, especially talk to customers.

Trust language:

- Outsource objectivity
- Remove yourself from the signal
- You are the bias
- Stop asking questions you already want answered

### Archetype 05: The Idea-Heavy, Action-Constrained

Volume lever.

Internal monologue:

> I have ideas, but I do not trust myself enough to act.

Trust language:

- Know before you build
- Signal to move forward, or stop
- Clarity before commitment

## Primary Targeting Decision

Prioritize the overlap between:

- Constrained Expert
- Scarred Operator

That is where willingness to pay, urgency, and credibility compound fastest.

Other archetypes are real, but they should not drive the first commercial surface.

## Trust Framework

Trustworthiness is a product of three things:

> Sincerity × Competence × Reliability = Trustworthiness

### Sincerity

We mean it.

FetchSignal does not hype, soften bad signal, or promise certainty it cannot deliver.

Sincerity signals that FetchSignal is on the builder's side, not selling them a fantasy.

### Competence

We know it.

Smart Idea Principals read methodology signals.

Competence is expressed through precision: respondent counts, sampling language, neutrality standards, and careful claims.

### Reliability

We deliver it.

Trust accumulates through kept promises.

Time stated means time honored.

Signal promised means signal delivered.

Outcome committed means outcome reported.

## Message Hierarchy

For Idea Principal-facing surfaces, communicate in this order:

1. Name the risk of false signal.
2. Make the idea feel protected, not exposed.
3. Explain that signal comes from real, independent people.
4. Clarify what the process does and does not promise.
5. Show how the output supports a decision.
6. Give a low-friction next step.

## Core Positioning Lines

Use selectively by surface.

- People are polite. That is not signal.
- Know before you build.
- Real signal from real people.
- Not friends. Not AI. Independent respondents with no reason to flatter you.
- Signal to move forward, or stop.
- The biggest risk is not being wrong. It is thinking you are right when you are not.
- Your idea might be worth everything. Or it might cost you a year.

## Signal Metaphor Usage

FetchSignal has two conceptual frames.

Do not mix both frames in the same headline.

### Frame A: Signal / Polling Frame

Use for awareness, hero sections, ads, social, and trust-building copy.

Best for explaining the high-level product promise.

Example:

> FetchSignal gives builders what Gallup gives politicians: real signal from real people before the build.

### Frame B: Gates / Verdict Frame

Use for methodology, mid-funnel education, sales deck explanation, and repeat usage.

Best for explaining how assumptions are tested.

Example:

> Every real business clears a set of gates. Miss one and you do not have a business yet. You have a hypothesis.

## LLM and AI Search Positioning

FetchSignal content should own the questions builders ask when they are uncertain.

Priority queries:

- How do I validate my startup idea before building?
- How do I know if people will pay for my idea?
- Should I build this product or am I wasting my time?
- What is market validation and how does it work?
- How do I talk to customers without bias?
- How do I get honest feedback on my startup idea?
- Is my startup idea good? How do I find out?
- How do I avoid building something nobody wants?

## Answer-First Copy Pattern

For SEO, LLM search, FAQs, and educational content, answer the user's question in the first sentence.

Bad:

> There are many ways to think about startup validation...

Better:

> The most reliable way to validate a startup idea is to test it with real, independent people who have no reason to flatter you.

Rules:

- Match the exact query phrasing.
- Sentence 1 equals the answer.
- Sentence 2 equals the elaboration.
- Use semantic HTML and FAQ schema where applicable.
- Educate first, brand second.

## Mobile-First Rules

Design for the thumb, not the cursor.

The likely first-touch visitor is a Constrained Expert searching at night on a phone.

### Thumb Rhythm

> Click. Read. Click. Read.

Rules:

- First visible headline should be the visitor's internal monologue, not a product description.
- Max 20 words between a tap target and the next decision point.
- Any content block over 3 lines on mobile should be an accordion.
- Label accordion headers as benefits, not categories.
- One primary CTA per screen.
- Primary CTA minimum height: 48px.
- Sticky primary CTA is acceptable on long-scroll pages.

## CTA Hierarchy

Every page should have:

1. One primary CTA
2. One secondary CTA
3. Optionally one ghost CTA

Never use two primary CTAs on the same screen.

### Primary CTA

Purpose:

> Conviction action.

Rules:

- Signal Blue fill
- White text
- 48px minimum height
- Outcome-framed copy

Examples:

- Start my signal test
- Start my signal check

### Secondary CTA

Purpose:

> Low-commitment path.

Rules:

- Transparent or bordered
- Never more prominent than primary

Examples:

- See how it works
- View a sample report

### Ghost CTA

Purpose:

> Skeptic path.

Rules:

- Text link only
- Below primary and secondary
- Use for methodology, FAQ, or learn-more content

Example:

- Learn about the methodology

## Respondent Engagement Rules

FetchSignal may use respondent-facing surfaces directly, but where Candid Voices exists, Candid Voices should generally be the respondent-facing trust layer.

Where this guide conflicts with the Candid Voices Style Guide on respondent-facing language, prefer Candid Voices for respondent surfaces.

FetchSignal-facing respondent language may still follow these rules internally:

- State the purpose immediately.
- Make the contribution feel consequential.
- State exact time.
- Never ask leading questions.
- Close with acknowledgment, not gratitude theater.
- Protect anonymity before the first question.

- Navy anchors authority.
- Amber signals the find.
- Parchment creates warmth.

- Never use teal.
- Never use Signal Blue as a full background.
- White text only on Navy or Signal Blue.
- Never Amber on Amber.

## Visual Motif

The signal bar is the primary visual motif.

It represents the moment real human voices translate into data.

Rules:

- Use signal bars as structural elements, not decoration.
- Bars should represent real numbers when shown in context.
- Do not show placeholder statistics as if real.
- Use Navy, Signal Blue, and Pulse Amber.

- Rounded caps
- Navy, Signal Blue, and Pulse Amber only

## Legal / Governance Guardrails

- Do not imply FetchSignal guarantees funding.
- Do not imply FetchSignal automatically leads to SwiftVC or Axiom Foundry.
- Do not imply positive signal guarantees success.
- Do not imply negative signal means failure.
- Do not imply data or study results are shared across entities by default.
- Do not expose the Idea Principal's idea carelessly.
- Do not overstate signal strength beyond the methodology.
- Do not fabricate respondent counts or placeholder statistics.
- Do not lead with Criterion Group in public customer-facing copy.
- Preserve customer-controlled sharing.

## Canonical Summary

FetchSignal is the trusted human-signal layer for Idea Principals who need truth before they build.

It should feel like Gallup-level seriousness applied to the founder's highest-risk decision: whether the idea deserves the next commitment.

The voice is authoritative, warm, direct, and precise.

The posture is trust before conversion.
